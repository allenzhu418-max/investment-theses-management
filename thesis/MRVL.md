# MRVL — Investment Thesis

## One-Sentence Thesis
MRVL is the connective tissue of AI infrastructure — its high-speed networking chips and storage controllers move data between GPUs, memory, and storage at the speeds AI requires, making it a direct beneficiary of both the AI compute boom and the memory boom.

## Why I Hold It
AI clusters don't just need GPUs and memory — they need chips that connect everything together at 400G/800G speeds. MRVL makes:
- **PAM4 DSPs and Ethernet switching silicon** — the chips that move data between GPUs inside AI clusters
- **SSD/HDD storage controllers** — directly tied to the data storage explosion driven by AI training datasets
- **Custom silicon (ASICs)** for hyperscalers — a growing but secondary revenue stream on top of the core networking/storage business

This makes MRVL less dependent on any single customer than it might appear, and tied to memory demand as much as compute demand.

## What Proves It's Working
- Data infrastructure (networking + storage) revenue growing >25% YoY
- Operating margins expanding toward 25%+ (currently 19% — meaningful room to run)
- Design win announcements at new AI cluster programmes
- Storage controller revenue growing alongside MU/SNDK memory demand

## What Breaks It
- High-speed networking commoditises — Broadcom or a new entrant displaces MRVL on price at a major hyperscaler
- Two consecutive quarters of data infrastructure revenue growth below 15%
- SSD controller market share loss to competing controller vendors

## Bear Case (read before adding)
MRVL has essentially no revenue yet from custom silicon at scale — it's a forward expectation trade. If hyperscaler custom chip programmes face delays (they always do), or volumes disappoint, the multiple compresses sharply. At RSI 96 and 23% above analyst consensus, the stock already prices in near-perfect execution for several years.
