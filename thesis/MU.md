# MU — Investment Thesis

## One-Sentence Thesis
MU is a leveraged bet on AI memory demand — specifically HBM for GPU stacks — and the question is whether this cycle has a structurally higher floor than prior DRAM cycles.

## Why I Hold It
AI training requires HBM attached to every GPU. MU is one of three companies globally that can make it (Samsung, SK Hynix, MU). Supply is constrained and pricing power is unusually strong for a memory company.

## The Open Question I'm Watching
Is memory truly non-cyclical now, or is this a particularly strong cycle that will eventually normalise? The answer resolves over the next 2–3 earnings cycles. **Gross margin trajectory is the single most important number** — if margins sustain above 50%, the structural thesis is holding; if they compress, it's cyclical.

## What Proves It's Working
- HBM revenue growing as % of total revenue each quarter
- Gross margins sustaining above 50% (current: 58%)
- MU calling out HBM supply sold out 12+ months forward on earnings calls

## What Breaks It
- Gross margin guidance drops two consecutive quarters
- Samsung or SK Hynix announces significant HBM capacity expansion that outpaces demand
- AI training efficiency gains (smaller models, less compute) reduce HBM demand growth

## Bear Case (read this when tempted to add)
Memory has always been cyclical because supply eventually catches demand. SK Hynix currently leads on HBM yield. If they or Samsung close the supply gap, pricing normalises and MU re-rates to a commodity multiple (P/E 8–10x vs current 31x trailing). The stock has already run past analyst targets.
