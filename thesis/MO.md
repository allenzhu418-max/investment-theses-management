# MO — Investment Thesis

## One-Sentence Thesis
MO is a cash flow machine in managed decline — combustible volumes fall predictably but oral nicotine (on! pouches) provides a growth offset, and the ~7.5–8% dividend yield compensates for the structural headwind.

## Why I Hold It
Defensive ballast and income. MO has raised its dividend for 55+ consecutive years. In a market selloff, it holds value while AI holdings decline. The oral nicotine category (on! pouches) is growing rapidly and partially replacing combustible revenue.

## What Proves It's Working
- Oral nicotine volume growth >20% YoY
- Dividend maintained and growing (the primary signal)
- Combustible volume declines staying within guided 4–6% annual range (not accelerating)

## What Breaks It
- FDA takes aggressive regulatory action against oral nicotine products
- Combustible volume declines accelerate beyond 8% annually
- **Dividend cut — the single most important signal** that the business model is under structural stress

## Bear Case (read before adding)
At $73, MO is at analyst consensus with limited further upside. Cigarette volumes are in structural decline. If oral nicotine regulation tightens (FDA has been unpredictable in this category), the growth offset disappears. Holding for yield is sound; adding at current levels has asymmetric downside.
