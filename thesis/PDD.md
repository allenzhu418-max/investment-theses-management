# PDD — Investment Thesis

## One-Sentence Thesis
PDD is deeply undervalued on fundamentals (P/E 10x, FCF $85B, 40% analyst upside) but the market applies a geopolitical discount that may be rational, not overdone — holding requires a clear view on whether the discount will eventually close.

## Why I Hold It
The gap between fundamentals and price is extreme. Temu's global expansion is real. The business generates exceptional free cash flow at scale. P/E 10x for a profitable, growing consumer internet company is historically cheap.

## The Open Question
Is the geopolitical discount permanent or temporary? This is not answerable from data alone — it requires a view on US-China relations, Temu's regulatory survival in Western markets, and China's domestic platform policy. **Coach guidance is especially important for this position.**

## What Proves It's Working
- Temu maintaining GMV growth in Western markets despite regulatory pressure
- China domestic Pinduoduo maintaining user growth vs Alibaba/JD
- Stock holding above $95 (52-week low support — the critical line)

## What Breaks It
- **Stock breaks below $95** — a new 52-week low on an already depressed price signals the market sees something beyond the valuation discount
- US government bans or severely restricts Temu operations
- China regulatory action against PDD (history of sudden platform crackdowns)

## Current Status: Under Active Review
Price $97 — only 1.6% above 52-week low. Falling on a day the market is up 600 points. This price action is a warning. The $95 level must hold. Without coach guidance on China macro/policy, this thesis is difficult to defend on price action alone.

## Bear Case (read before adding)
Chinese companies report under accounting standards with less transparency than US peers. The $85B FCF figure deserves scrutiny — Temu is burning cash on subsidies to gain GMV. Geopolitical risk is not a temporary discount; it could worsen materially if US-China trade tensions escalate. ADR delisting risk is non-zero.
