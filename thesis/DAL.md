# DAL — Investment Thesis

## One-Sentence Thesis
DAL is the highest-quality airline in a structurally recovering industry — premium travel demand is resilient, and its SkyMiles loyalty programme and domestic dominance create a durable competitive moat at a cheap valuation.

## Why I Hold It
9x forward P/E for a consistently profitable, cash-generative business with strong competitive position. The market prices airlines as commodities — DAL deserves a premium. Premium cabin and international routes are structurally higher margin. Loyalty programme is a financial services business embedded in an airline.

## What Proves It's Working
- Revenue per available seat mile (RASM) growing or stable
- Operating margin sustaining above 10%
- Loyalty programme (SkyMiles) revenue growing as % of total
- Fuel costs declining or effectively hedged

## What Breaks It
- Oil price spike above $100/barrel sustained for a quarter
- Recession causing premium travel demand to fall (business class is the margin engine)
- Major operational crisis (safety incident, IT failure — DAL had a major IT outage in 2024)
- RASM declining for two consecutive quarters

## Bear Case (read before adding)
Airlines are cyclical and capital-intensive. DAL's balance sheet carries significant debt. A recession scenario cuts earnings sharply and the stock re-rates from 9x to 6x — a 33% decline on an earnings miss. The Iran deal catalyst (May 2026) is event-driven and fully reversible if geopolitics deteriorate.
