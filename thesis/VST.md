# VST — Investment Thesis

## One-Sentence Thesis
VST is an AI power infrastructure play — data centres need gigawatts of reliable power, and Vistra operates gas and nuclear plants positioned to sign long-term offtake contracts with hyperscalers at premium prices.

## Why I Hold It
Power demand for AI is structural and long-dated. Nuclear plants are effectively irreplaceable — impossible to build new ones quickly. VST owns operational capacity that hyperscalers desperately need and cannot easily replicate. 45% analyst upside to consensus — highest in the portfolio.

## What Proves It's Working
- Announcement of long-term power purchase agreements (PPAs) with data centre operators
- Q1 earnings guidance raise on power demand outlook (earnings imminent as of 2026-05-06)
- Retail power margins holding stable while wholesale contracts expand

## What Breaks It
- Hyperscalers pivot to on-site solar/wind over grid power at scale
- Federal energy regulation changes that cap wholesale power pricing
- Nuclear plant reliability issues (unplanned outages)
- FCF remains negative beyond 2026 — capex not converting to cash

## Near-Term Catalyst
Q1 earnings imminent (as of 2026-05-06). Stock is coiling at mid-Bollinger Band with neutral RSI — a classic pre-catalyst setup. Earnings will likely resolve direction.

## Bear Case (read before adding)
VST is down 27% from its 52-week high — the market has partially lost faith. FCF is negative. The AI power narrative is speculative until actual PPAs are signed at disclosed prices. Energy policy risk (rate caps, permitting changes) is real and can materialise quickly.
